GST Filing

Goods and Service Tax (GST) is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services. This law has replaced many indirect tax laws that previously existed in India. The Goods and Service Tax Act was passed in the Parliament on 29th March 2017. The Act came into effect on 1st July 2017. GST is one indirect tax for the entire country.

The registered taxpayers are mandatorily to file their returns periodically to tax administrative authorities. In their return, the taxpayer needs to disclose the details of Purchase, tax on purchase, Sales and tax on Sales of particular month. The tax liability is auto calculated in GST system and if any tax amount to be paid in cash over and above after setting off input tax credit (Tax on Purchase).

These returns are as per the CGST Act*

Late filing Fees of GST Returns

Return filing is mandatory under GST. It means that even if there is no transaction, Nil return to be filed A return cannot be filed unless the previous month/quarter’s returns were not filed. late fee of Rs. 50/day i.e. Rs. 25 per day in each case of CGST and SGST (in case of any tax liability) and Rs. 20/day i.e. Rs. 10/- day in each CGST and SGST (in case of Nil tax liability) subject to a maximum of Rs. 5000/-, from the given due date to the actual date when the returns are finally filed.

Interest on Late payment of GST

According to the GST Penalty regulations, an interest will be charged at the rate of 18 percent per annum from the taxpayers who fail to pay their taxes on time. The interest will be levied for the days after the due date.
